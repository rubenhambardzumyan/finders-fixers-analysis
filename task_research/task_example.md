---

**DOK3 Insight:**
Current curriculum creates cognitive overload because DOK levels are taught as isolated units with no practice between introductions. When working memory's 4-element capacity meets high element interactivity in massed presentation, cognitive overload is guaranteed. Solution requires distributed reinforcement across 15+ lessons rather than massed introduction.

**Relevance:** This insight identifies a critical flaw in current course structure and specifies the required fix - distributed practice is essential for effective direct instruction that respects cognitive constraints.

---

**DOK3 Insight:**
Worked examples provide 6x time advantage over problem-solving for novice learners, but this advantage disappears completely when annotations direct attention to wrong patterns or fail to eliminate split-attention effects.

**Relevance:** This insight reveals that the quality of direct instruction components (specifically worked examples) is as critical as their presence - poorly designed direct instruction can negate its benefits entirely.

---

**DOK3 Insight:**
Scaffolding must follow specific progression: present complete worked examples first, then gradually remove support, and only introduce variation after learners achieve 60% accuracy on similar examples. Removing scaffolding too early causes reversion to aimless content generation.

**Relevance:** This specifies the precise sequencing required for effective direct instruction scaffolding in the course structure, ensuring learner progression maximizes outcomes.

---

**DOK2 Summary:**
Direct Instruction has three main organizational components: (1) Program Design - content analysis, clear communication, instructional formats, skill sequencing, teaching generalizable strategies; (2) Organization of Instruction - track-based organization, grouping by zone of proximal development, flexible grouping; (3) Student-Teacher Interaction - scripted wording and corrections, continuous assessment, immediate reinforcement.

**Relevance:** This framework defines the comprehensive structure for implementing direct instruction across all levels of course design, from content to delivery.

Cohort program structure specified as: 2-3 week duration with daily sessions, limited to 10 people per cohort, focus on creating 3-5 high-quality BrainLifts per participant, includes one-on-one coaching access, requires manager involvement and stakeholder alignment.

**Relevance:** This describes the concrete course structure that implements direct instruction principles in a scalable cohort model for the BrainLift platform.

**Relevance:** This insight explains why synchronous presentation of related information is essential in course materials - asynchronous presentation wastes working memory capacity on retention rather than learning.
