## Purpose Statement

### Issue 1: Vague Metrics Definition

#### Feedback

The purpose mentions "increase community activity" and "increase content creation" but provides no baseline metrics or targets. What does "increased activity" mean - 10% more posts? 50% more interactions? Without specific numbers, you can't measure success.

#### Coaching

Define three concrete metrics with targets: 1) User engagement rate (from X% to Y%), 2) Content creation volume (increase by Z posts/day), 3) Migration success rate (X% of users successfully migrated). Track these weekly to prove Playbox delivers value.

### Issue 2: Technical Focus Over Business Impact

#### Feedback

The purpose emphasizes technical unification ("Unite Cloud-only Playbox gamification with Jive Unity") but doesn't explain the business problem this solves. Why does unification matter to end users or the business beyond reducing maintenance?

#### Coaching

Reframe from user impact: "Playbox solves the 40% user engagement drop during Cloud-to-Unity migrations by preserving gamification continuity." Connect every technical decision to user retention, revenue, or operational cost metrics.

## DOK4 SPOVs

### Issue 1: Not Controversial Enough

#### Feedback

"Gamification must feel fair, fast and visible" is widely accepted best practice, not a spiky point of view. Any gamification expert would agree with this statement, making it common sense rather than new knowledge.

#### Coaching

Create a truly spiky stance: "Gamification should be unfair by design - top performers need exponentially greater rewards than average users to create aspirational dynamics." This forces readers to pick sides and challenges conventional wisdom.

### Issue 2: Lacks Actionable Specificity

#### Feedback

"Zero-tolerance for data loss" is a quality standard, not an actionable SPOV that guides specific decisions. It doesn't tell you HOW to achieve this or what trade-offs to make when perfect fidelity conflicts with other goals.

#### Coaching

Make it actionable: "Accept 5% data loss for 50% faster migrations - users value quick transitions over perfect history preservation. Run parallel systems for 30 days then hard cut-over." This SPOV drives specific architectural decisions.

### Issue 3: Missing Business Trade-offs

#### Feedback

The SPOVs focus on technical perfection without addressing business realities like cost, timeline, or resource constraints. "Multi-tenant Data Isolation" is an implementation detail, not a strategic stance.

#### Coaching

Add business-focused SPOVs: "Ship imperfect gamification in 2 weeks rather than perfect gamification in 2 months - engagement decay during migration outweighs feature completeness." This guides priority decisions when facing real constraints.
