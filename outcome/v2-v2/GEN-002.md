**Poor Performance**: GEN-002 shows concerning results with only 4 out of 15 analyzable BrainLifts passing (27% success rate). 11 BrainLifts failed this assessment, with 1 being unanalyzable due to missing content. This is the worst-performing finder among all assessed.

**Key Success Patterns**:
- **Deep Cross-Domain Integration**: The few successful BrainLifts extensively incorporated insights from multiple external domains
- **Fundamental Assumption Challenge**: Winners used external knowledge to question basic domain assumptions that practitioners take for granted
- **Multiple Domain Sources**: Successful BrainLifts drew from 4-6 different external domains rather than staying within related fields
- **Practical Application**: Winners connected external insights to specific domain challenges and decisions

**Failure Points**:
- **Domain Insularity**: Failed BrainLifts stayed within their primary domain or closely related fields
- **Surface-Level External References**: Some failures mentioned other domains without deeply integrating their insights
- **Comfort Zone Adherence**: Most BrainLifts avoided challenging fundamental domain assumptions using external perspectives
- **Lack of Cross-Domain Expertise**: Authors appeared to lack deep enough knowledge in external domains to make meaningful connections

**Best Practice Examples**:
- **MarTech SMB Acquisition**: Integrated private equity, consumer psychology, AI/ML, and services industry insights to challenge MarTech orthodoxy
- **RCA BrainLift**: Combined aviation safety, psychology, nuclear industry practices, and systems theory to revolutionize incident analysis thinking

**Critical Gap Identification**: 
The high failure rate reveals a systemic issue: most BrainLift authors lack the cross-domain knowledge needed to challenge their domain orthodoxy effectively. This suggests:
- Authors need coaching on identifying relevant external domains
- BrainLift methodology should include explicit cross-domain research requirements
- Expert panels should include cross-domain authorities, not just domain specialists

**Overall Assessment**: The GEN-002 finder identifies the most significant weakness in current BrainLift quality. The overwhelming failure rate indicates that truly innovative thinking requires cross-domain expertise that most authors currently lack. This represents the highest-impact area for BrainLift improvement, as cross-domain insights are essential for challenging orthodox thinking and creating genuinely innovative perspectives that advance domains beyond conventional wisdom.
