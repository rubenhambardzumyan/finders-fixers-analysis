## GEN-001 Assessment

**Status**: ‚ùå FAILED  
**Result**: This BrainLift does not sufficiently challenge widely accepted assumptions about employee engagement platforms or enterprise software management.

### Feedback
The SPOVs presented are largely operational and technical recommendations rather than controversial positions that challenge fundamental assumptions about employee engagement, platform design, or enterprise software strategy. Most practitioners would readily agree with positions like "forced upgrades are essential" or "technical debt needs addressing."

### Coaching
To strengthen this BrainLift, challenge fundamental assumptions such as:
- **DOK3 Insight**: Question whether employee engagement platforms actually improve engagement or just create measurement theater
- **DOK2 Summary**: Challenge the assumption that internal communication platforms add value over external tools like Slack or Teams
- **DOK1 Fact**: Question whether enterprise software should prioritize feature richness over simplicity and user experience

Consider positions that would make enterprise software experts uncomfortable, such as arguing that most employee engagement platforms harm authentic workplace culture or that technical debt is sometimes strategically valuable for customer lock-in.
