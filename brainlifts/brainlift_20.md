# Purpose

- The purpose of this BrainLift is to create the adoption strategy of the BrainLift Coach product so that it helps users to create great BrainLifts. The goal of this BrainLift is to acquire 500 active (those users who make at least one change in their BrainLift every day) Trilogy users (engineers and product managers first) in 30 days from the moment of the official launch.

# DOK4 - SPOVs

- **[4.1] DOK4 - SPOV: **Software companies with traditional knowledge management that are not embracing the concept of BrainLifting are doomed to extinction.
- **[4.2] DOK4 - SPOV: **BrainLift adoption requires starting with power users who become internal champions before expanding, rather than attempting broad rollout that risks high churn from users feeling overwhelmed.
- **[4.3] DOK4 - SPOV: **Effective onboarding must focus on making users feel they can "win" with BrainLifts rather than just demonstrating features, addressing emotional barriers that cause churn when users feel "dumb, overwhelmed, or like outsiders."

# DOK3 - Insights

- **[3.1] DOK3 - Insight:** Successful product adoption requires a clearly defined user journey with objective metrics focused on customer value rather than feature usage, allowing product managers to identify where users get stuck and proactively address gaps.
- **[3.2] DOK3 - Insight: **Limiting initial user numbers until demonstrating success with a small cohort prevents "burning users like a meat grinder," creating a foundation of success stories before opening adoption floodgates.
- **[3.3] DOK3 - Insight: **User feedback mechanisms must be designed for definitive rather than inferred signals, incorporating simple binary options that capture clear user sentiment rather than relying on ambiguous engagement metrics.
